Antiquity and the Middle Ages the territory was originally inhabited by Germanic tribes and later became part of the ROMAN EMPIRE after tha fall af Rome, the region was divided into counties and duchies During the middle Ages, it was part of the Holy Roman Empire. Important trading cities emerged, such as Amsterdam, Utrecht, and Rotterdam.
Spanish rule and the struggle for i9ndependence (16th-17th centuries) In the 16th century, the Netherlands came under the rule of the Spanish Monarchy (Charles V and Philip II).

Major tensions arose due to:

  • Heavy taxation

  • Centralization of power

  • Persecution of Protestantism (Calvinism)

 These tensions led to the Eighty Years’ War (1568–1648). In 1648, Spain officially recognized the independence of the Dutch Republic (United Provinces).
The Dutch Golden Age /17th century) The Netherlands became a major maritime and commercial power. Strong development of banking, international trade, and colonial companies (such as the VOC). Cultural and scientific flourishing with figures like Rembrandt and Vermeer. Creation of a colonial empire in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
18th and 19th centuries Economic decline compared to Great Britain and France. French influence and occupation during the Napoleonic Wars. In 1815, the Kingdom of the Netherlands was established.

  • In 1830, Belgium gained independence.
20th century Neutral during World War I. Occupied by Nazi Germany during World War II (1940–1945). Loss of colonies, especially Indonesia

  • Post-war reconstruction and strong economic growth.
