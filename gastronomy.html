DUTCH CUISINE

Dutch cuisine is characterized by its simplicity, the use of fresh ingredients, and a variety of traditional dishes that reflect its history and culture.
Common Ingredients
Dutch cooking uses basic ingredients such as potatoes, bread, meat, fish, and dairy. Cheeses are especially notable, with famous varieties like Gouda and Edam. In addition, fish from the North Sea, such as herring and mussels, is a staple in the local diet.

Typical Dishes
Stamppot: This is the national dish, a mashed potato casserole mixed with vegetables like kale or carrots, often served with a smoked sausage.
Erwtensoep: A thick green pea soup, cooked with pork and vegetables, ideal for cold days.
Bitterballen: A popular bar snack, similar to croquettes, filled with meat and spices, served with mustard.
Haring: Raw herring traditionally eaten, often accompanied by onions and pickles.
Stroopwafels: A typical sweet, consisting of two layers of dough with a syrup filling in the middle, very popular as a snack.
Pannenkoeken: Large pancakes that can be sweet or savory, often served with a variety of toppings.

Cultural Influences
The gastronomy of the Netherlands has also been influenced by its colonial history, incorporating flavors from Indonesia, such as Rijsttafel, which is a meal that includes a variety of small dishes. In addition, the brewing tradition is strong, with brands such as Heineken and Amstel being known internationally.

The cuisine of the Netherlands is rich and varied, offering a mix of local flavors and international influences. Trying the typical dishes is a great way to get to know the culture and history of the country.
